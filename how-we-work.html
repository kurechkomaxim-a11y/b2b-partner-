The process

From brief to delivered site.

One sequence, one counterpart. We show you exactly where responsibility sits at each step.

Eight steps

The full process.

  1. Project brief

    Specification, BOQ, drawings, or a written description. We clarify volume, finish, tolerances, delivery point, target timing, and budget envelope.

  2. Sourcing screen (24–48 hours)

    We screen the supplier graph against your brief. Two to three realistic paths, each with factory-level pricing and lead time.

  3. Landed quote

    Full itemised pricing: factory price, freight, customs, insurance, our fee. Payment milestones and Incoterms defined upfront.

  4. Sample and specification lock-in

    Physical sample shipped against the chosen option. Specification, tolerances, and acceptance criteria signed off in writing before we commit to the order.

  5. Contract and deposit

    Contract signed. 30% deposit triggers order placement with the factory.

  6. Pre-shipment inspection

    Independent inspection at the factory before loading — photos, videos, dimensional check, packaging audit. Report sent to you for release.

  7. Logistics and customs

    Freight coordination, export documents, customs clearance at destination, insurance coverage for the full transit.

  8. Delivery on site

    Delivery to your site or specified warehouse. Full document pack handed over. Defect window opens — claims process defined in the contract.

Risk-pack

What we take on, what stays with you.

What we take on

  • Factory selection against the specification
  • Pre-shipment inspection and acceptance criteria
  • International freight and route planning
  • Customs clearance and document coordination
  • Cargo insurance for full transit value
  • Defined claims process with response windows

What stays with you

  • Site readiness and off-loading
  • Any works and installation on site
  • Force majeure — weather, border, political events
  • Post-delivery storage and handling
  • Specification changes after lock-in (change orders)

FAQ

Common questions from procurement and project teams.

We already have stone suppliers.
We don't replace your existing channel — we complement it for projects where it's slow, unclear, or expensive. Many of our clients keep their local supplier and use us for specific projects where sourcing flexibility matters.
Why work with an intermediary?
We're not a broker — we're an external procurement function for one narrow category. You're paying for sourcing depth, inspection, document handling, and one-point accountability. If those don't matter for a given project, you don't need us.
Who's responsible when something goes wrong?
Responsibility is defined in the contract for every stage — factory defect, transit damage, document error, delivery delay. Claims process, response times, and remedies are written down before you sign.
Will it actually be cheaper?
Sometimes yes, often comparable. We don't sell price — we sell landed-price clarity and lower risk. If price is the only criterion, a local distributor may win on small volumes. On project volumes with specification complexity, we usually compete on total cost including risk.
Why should we trust a new channel?
Start small. First engagement can be a single sample order, a single lot, or a pilot shipment — with no commitment beyond that project. Track record is built, not promised.
Do you only work on large volumes?
Our wedge is project-based work in the 500–2,000 m² range, where sourcing effort justifies itself. We do handle smaller one-offs if they fit into an existing shipment or sourcing cycle.
